How to calculate tap drill size

The tap drill formula for metric threads: Tap Drill = Major Diameter - Pitch (for ~75% thread). For exact percentage: Tap Drill = Major Diameter - (Pitch × Thread% / 76.98).

For imperial (UNC/UNF) threads: Tap Drill = Major Diameter - (1 / TPI) for 75% thread.

Why 75% thread?

A 75% thread engagement provides approximately 95% of the strength of a 100% thread, while requiring significantly less tapping torque. Going beyond 75% adds very little strength but dramatically increases the risk of tap breakage. For most applications, 65-75% is optimal.

Helicoil (thread insert) drilling

Helicoil inserts require a larger drill than standard tapping because the insert itself provides the thread. The STI (Screw Thread Insert) drill size is typically the nominal thread size + 0.25 to 0.50mm depending on thread pitch.
