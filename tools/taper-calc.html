Taper calculations for CNC programming

A taper is a gradual decrease in diameter along the length of a workpiece. The taper angle is critical for CNC lathe programming, tool holder fitting, and precision machining operations.

Key formulas

Taper per foot (TPF) = (D - d) × 12 / L (where D, d, L are in inches)
Taper per inch (TPI) = (D - d) / L
Included angle = 2 × arctan((D - d) / (2 × L))
Half angle = arctan((D - d) / (2 × L))
Taper ratio = L / (D - d) expressed as 1:N

Morse tapers

Morse tapers are the most common standard taper used in drill press spindles, lathe tailstocks, and milling machine spindles. They range from MT0 to MT7, with MT2 and MT3 being most common in workshop machines. The approximate taper rate is 5/8 inch per foot (~1:20 ratio).
